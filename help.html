Frequently Asked Questions

To view the DWGA (Dynamic Water Governance Atlas) map:

  1. Navigate to the DWGA section from the main menu
  2. Select your region of interest from the dropdown menu
  3. Choose the data layers you wish to display
  4. Use the zoom and pan tools to explore the map
  5. Click on any location to view detailed information

Data download is available for registered users. Follow these steps:

  1. Go to the Data Download section
  2. Select the dataset category you're interested in
  3. Choose your preferred format (CSV, JSON, or Shapefile)
  4. Define the geographic area and time period
  5. Click the "Download" button

Note: Some datasets may require special permissions or may be subject to usage restrictions.

SWR (Sustainable Water Resources) recommendations are based on comprehensive analysis of water availability, usage patterns, and environmental factors. Key elements include:

  • Water Allocation Priorities: Suggested distribution across sectors
  • Conservation Measures: Efficiency improvements and reduction strategies
  • Risk Assessment: Identification of vulnerable areas and periods
  • Implementation Timeline: Short, medium, and long-term actions

Each recommendation includes supporting data and rationale for informed decision-making.

DWGA (Dynamic Water Governance Atlas) and GWS (Groundwater Sustainability) serve different but complementary purposes:

DWGA
  • Focuses on surface water resources
  • Provides governance and policy information
  • Includes administrative boundaries and regulations
  • Dynamic mapping of water governance structures
GWS
  • Concentrates on groundwater resources
  • Assesses aquifer health and sustainability
  • Includes recharge rates and extraction data
  • Projects future groundwater availability
